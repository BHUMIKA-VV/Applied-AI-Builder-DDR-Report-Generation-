INTRODUCTION
------------
A thermal imaging survey was conducted to identify moisture, heat loss, and electrical
anomalies not visible to the naked eye. All images are referenced in the findings below.
Thermal images were captured under steady-state conditions after sunset to maximize
Delta-T across the building envelope.

====================================================
FINDING T-01: MAIN HALL CEILING – ACTIVE MOISTURE
====================================================
  Thermal Image : THERM_001.jpg
  Area          : Main hall ceiling, central zone
  Findings      : Clear cold spot anomaly detected in the central ceiling zone,
                  consistent with trapped moisture or saturated insulation.
                  Temperature differential: Surface 22.1°C vs surrounding 26.4°C
                  (delta 4.3°C). Pattern matches water migration from roof membrane
                  failure above.
  Confidence    : High – pattern consistent with active leak
  Cross-ref     : Corroborates Obs 1.1 (visual staining and damp tiles)

====================================================
FINDING T-02: EAST CORRIDOR CEILING – MOISTURE MIGRATION
====================================================
  Thermal Image : THERM_002.jpg
  Area          : East corridor ceiling, full length
  Findings      : Linear cold pattern along the ceiling-wall junction of the east
                  corridor. Min temp recorded: 21.8°C; ambient 26.4°C. This suggests
                  moisture tracking along the slab soffit from the roof above.
  Confidence    : High
  Cross-ref     : Corroborates Obs 1.2 (efflorescence and paint peeling)

====================================================
FINDING T-03: NORTH FAÇADE – THERMAL BRIDGING AT CRACK LOCATIONS
====================================================
  Thermal Image : THERM_003.jpg
  Area          : North external wall (viewed internally)
  Findings      : Thermal bridges visible at two crack locations. Temperature at
                  crack zones: 20.3°C vs wall face 25.1°C. Indicates compromised
                  insulation continuity. No significant moisture trapped in wall
                  cavity detected at this time.
  NOTE          : Structural assessment required – thermal imaging alone cannot
                  confirm settlement. Refer to structural engineer.
  Confidence    : Medium (moisture aspect); structural interpretation not possible
                  from thermal alone.
  Cross-ref     : Partially corroborates Obs 2.1

====================================================
FINDING T-04: SERVER ROOM EAST WALL – SEVERE MOISTURE
====================================================
  Thermal Image : THERM_004.jpg, THERM_005.jpg
  Area          : Server room, east wall (lower zone)
  Findings      : Significant cold anomaly across entire lower 700mm of east wall.
                  Min temp: 19.2°C vs ambient 24.0°C (delta 4.8°C). Pattern is
                  consistent with rising damp or active moisture ingress from floor
                  level. HVAC condensate drain failure likely contributing source.
  NOTE          : Thermographer notes that visual inspection report states 600mm
                  rising damp height; thermal imaging indicates up to 700mm in some
                  zones — slight discrepancy.
  Confidence    : Very High
  Cross-ref     : Corroborates Obs 3.1 and Obs 5.2

====================================================
FINDING T-05: IT ROOM CEILING VOID – ELECTRICAL HOTSPOT
====================================================
  Thermal Image : THERM_006.jpg
  Area          : IT room, above suspended ceiling (cable runs)
  Findings      : Two localised hotspots detected in cable run area.
                  Hotspot A: 54.3°C (cable junction/connection point)
                  Hotspot B: 48.7°C (separate run, approx. 1.2m from hotspot A)
                  Both temperatures significantly above acceptable threshold of 40°C
                  above ambient for electrical components.
  RISK LEVEL    : CRITICAL – immediate isolation and inspection by qualified
                  electrician required. Fire risk present.
  Confidence    : Very High
  Cross-ref     : Corroborates Obs 5.1 (visual exposed/damaged wiring)

====================================================
FINDING T-06: STORAGE ROOM B – COLD BRIDGING
====================================================
  Thermal Image : THERM_007.jpg
  Area          : Storage Room B, north-west corner (internal)
  Findings      : Classic cold triangle pattern at internal corner, indicating cold
                  bridging through external wall. Min temp 19.7°C vs room 24.1°C.
                  This creates a surface below dew point, causing condensation and
                  subsequent mould growth.
  Confidence    : High
  Cross-ref     : Corroborates Obs 3.2 (black mould growth)

====================================================
FINDING T-07: RECEPTION FLOOR TILES – NO THERMAL ANOMALY
====================================================
  Thermal Image : THERM_008.jpg
  Area          : Reception area floor
  Findings      : No significant thermal anomaly detected beneath floor tiles. No
                  moisture beneath tile bed identified via thermal. The hollow tiles
                  (Obs 4.1) appear to be an adhesion failure only, not moisture-related.
  Confidence    : High
  Cross-ref     : Provides additional context for Obs 4.1

====================================================
FINDING T-08: HVAC UNIT (SERVER ROOM) – CONDENSATE LEAK CONFIRMED
====================================================
  Thermal Image : THERM_009.jpg
  Area          : Server room ceiling, HVAC unit
  Findings      : HVAC unit shows cold zone below unit body consistent with condensate
                  water accumulation and drip path. Blockage in condensate drain line
                  confirmed thermally. Contributing to moisture load on east wall (T-04).
  Confidence    : High
  Cross-ref     : Corroborates Obs 5.2
